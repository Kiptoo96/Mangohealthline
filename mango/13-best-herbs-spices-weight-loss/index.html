1. Cinnamon

Cinnamon is a weight loss spice that is especially beneficial for those with type II diabetes because of its strong blood sugar-lowering properties.

Maintaining low blood sugar levels is essential for having clear, consistent energy all day. When too many carbohydrates are consumed, excessive insulin spikes can be reduced with the use of cinnamon.


You may include more cinnamon in your diet by eating it on lean chicken breasts, in a bowl of oats, coffee, or whey protein shakes!

2. Cayenne

Cayenne contains a compound called capsaicin, which has two powerful weight loss benefits:

  • Burns fat off the body by increasing metabolism
  • Helps to suppress appetite and curb cravings

Almost all hot sauces (Sriracha, Lousiana hot sauce, and Tony Chachere’s) contain cayenne pepper in them.  You can also opt for 100% cayenne pepper, just make sure you don’t burn your tongue!  It’s VERY hot!

3. Ginseng

Ginseng is an herbal root found in Asia and some parts of North America boasting a few powerful benefits:

  • Helps prevent heart disease
  • Lowers blood sugar levels
  • Boosts mood
  • Helps with fatigue
  • Stimulates weight loss

The most common way to get the root in is an herbal tea.  Be careful if you are stimulant sensitive, and start with a “weaker” tea if in the beginning.

4. Peppermint 

When it comes to appetite, peppermint has an oddly strong effect that helps people lose weight. Specifically, one study found that those who were instructed to sniff peppermint when they were hungry consumed 1800 fewer calories in a week than people who were not.

Although I don't discuss essential oils much, they could be a useful resource for obtaining in a highly concentrated dose of peppermint.

Use the oils or make some peppermint tea, and make sure to inhale a few times before drinking.

5. Dandelions

A dandelion herb's entire body is edible and full of nutrients. They are rich in beta-carotene, vitamin K1, dietary fiber, and numerous other vitamins and minerals.

Additionally demonstrated to help decrease cholesterol, aid restore blood sugar balance, and increase feelings of fullness following a meal is dandelion tea.

6. Turmeric

This yellow-orange spice aids with weight loss in a number of ways.

Its expertise lies in lowering the blood vessels required to create adipose tissue, therefore minimizing inflammation in that area. In addition, it tastes very good, and we like to include it in our normal meals.


Serve it with fried chicken, eggs, potatoes, or in a fragrant tea.

7. Garlic

In addition to improving the flavor of meals, fresh garlic has been demonstrated to improve the body's ability to metabolize fats and carbs. Garlic has even been demonstrated in certain trials to stop the body from generating fat.

Add it to food in powdered or fresh (minced) form.

8. Cloves

Another spice that's essential for a healthy kitchen is cloves, which can increase metabolism and aid in calorie burning. They have a high nutritional content and anti-inflammatory properties.

They go great with sweet potatoes and turkey.

9. Mustard

Due to the favorable thermogenic impact of mustard seeds, the body naturally burns more calories.

Instead of using the typical yellow mustard sauce that is sometimes linked with ketchup—which is loaded with sodium—try flavoring food with dry mustard powder.


It gives a subtle kick and an intriguing flavor!

10. Cardamom

This is another interesting spice that helps boost metabolism and has been shown to lower blood glucose levels.  Keeping blood glucose levels down keeps insulin low and the body’s ability to burn fat HIGH.

Cardamom comes in either whole form or powdered, but I recommend powdered for ease of use. Try adding it to chicken to start with.  Some people even add it to coffee!

11. Parsley

Parsley has multiple positive effects on weight loss:

  • Helps control appetite and suppress cravings
  • Reduces the amount of sugar in the blood
  • Helps the body better break down and absorb healthy nutrients

It goes great in salads, green smoothies, and on lean meats like pork and chicken.

12. Cumin 

Both the immune system and the digestive system benefit from cumin. It increases energy and reduces hunger by facilitating the body's more effective nutrition processing.

Add cumin to salsa, rice, chili, and vegetable soups.

13. Black pepper

Most people have it and never use it!  Here are some reasons why you should…

  • Contains capsaicin for appetite control and hunger suppression
  • Helps the body burn more calories through high amounts of piperine
  • Improves digestion
  • Helps burn fat at a faster rate

It’s yet another spice that can have big effects when consistently consumed.
